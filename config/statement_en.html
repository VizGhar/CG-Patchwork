The Goal

Become the master of patchworking by filling your quilt canvas with patches. Be careful! Every patch costs you a few Buttons and it also takes some Time to sew it on your canvas. Those are your most valuable resources

This game is heavily inspired by 2 player board game Patchwork.

  Rules

This is turn based game but players do not necessarily alternate between turns. The player who is furthest behind on the Timeline takes his turn. This may result in a player taking multiple turns in a row before his opponent can take one. If there are both time tokens on same spot, player that played last move takes his turn.

Your goal is to collect more Buttons than your opponent and to cover as much of your quilt board with patches as possible. Uncovered squares on quilt board will cost you 2 Buttons per piece at the end of game.

On your turn, you carry out one of the following actions:
  • Advance and Receive Buttons using SKIP command
  • Take and Place a Patch using PLAY command

Advance and Receive Buttons - the SKIP command

Your Time token will move on the Timeline so that it occupies the space directly in front of your opponent's time token. You receive 1 Button per space your time token moves. You can do so using SKIP command.

Take and Place a Patch - the PLAY command

This action consists of a few step:
  • Choose from the three available Patches and place it on board
  • Pay the depicted number of Buttons to the supply
  • Move your Time token on the time board by a number of spaces as depicted on the label
Patches can be rotated in any way - flipped (horizontally) and/or rotated right (clockwise) 0 , 1 , 2 or 3 times. It can be placed anywhere on board as far as it won't cover already placed patches. First player to fully cover 7x7 square of quill board will receive Special Patch for bonus points at the End of the game.

Timeline

Timeline consists of starting point and 53 time points. Regardless of the action you take, you always move your time token on the time board. Some spaces of the time board are marked (WIP - visual not yet ready). Whenever you move onto or past one of those spaces, resolve the corresponding event:
  • Special Patch: PLAY the special patch and place it on your quilt board. The special patches are the only way to "patch" single spaces of your quilt board. If you receive the Special Patch, you will have to resolve it in next turn. Playing special patch cannot be SKIP -ped
  • Button Income: You receive a number of buttons according to the patches on your quilt board.

  End of the game and scoring

The game ends after both time tokens reach the last point of the Timeline. If a time token were to move past the last space, it simply stops on the last space. In case of SKIP action, you only receive buttons for the actual number of spaces moved.

Starting with 200 points
  • Add 1 point for each Button you have left
  • Add 7 if you own Special Tile
  • Subtract 2 points for each empty space of your quilt board
The player with the higher score wins. In case of a tie, the player who got to the final space of the time board first wins. (WIP - quite rare, not implemented yet)
Victory Conditions
  • You have more points at the end of the game or
  • You have same amount of points at the end of the game but you got to final space first
Defeat Conditions
  • Unknown command (only SKIP or PLAY are allowed)
  • SKIP while holding Special Patch
  • PLAY unavailable or non-existent patch
  • PLAY patch with invalid placement (outside quilt board/overlaping other patch)
  • PLAY patch you have no money for

  Game Input/Output

Initialization Input

Line 1: 1 integer income_events how many "Button Income" events are there on Time Board

Line 2: income_events integers of income_time when the "Button income" event will occur

Line 3: 1 integer patch_events how many "Special Patch" events are there on Time Board

Line 4: patch_events integers of patch_time when the "Special Patch" event will occur

Line 5: tiles - count of tiles - always 33

Next tiles lines: 5 values: tile_id tile_earning tile_button_price tile_time_price tile_shape

Game turn Input

Line 1: 2 integers my_buttons = amount of buttons you currently own; and my_time = how far is your token on Time Board

Line 2: 3 integers opponent_buttons = amount of buttons your opponent currently owns; opponent_time = how far is your opponent's token on Time Board

Line 3-11: 9 lines representing opponent board - O is taken field . is empty

Line 12: 1 integer playable_tiles = amount of tiles you can play (either 3 during normal turn or 1 while resolving "Special Patch" event)

Next playable_tiles lines: 5 values: tile_id tile_earning tile_button_price tile_time_price tile_shape

To describe all fields of tile:
  • tile_id - 0 - 32 for standard tiles or negative for Special Patch
  • tile_earning - how much will this patch earn during each "Button income" event
  • tile_button_price - how much buttons this patch costs
  • tile_time_price - how much time this patch costs
  • tile_shape - single string representing tile shape as follows: | separates rows of patch. Each rows consists of . and O characters. O means current square is taken by this patch
Output for one Game Turn
A single line with either of 2 actions:
  • SKIP
  • PLAY tile_id rotations horizontal_flip x y
Output for one Game Turn
Example of usage:
PLAY
tile_id
rotate - how many times to rotate tile clockwise
horizontal_flip - 1 if tile should be flipped horizontally first
x and y - coordinates of top/left corner after applying transformations

PLAY 3 1 2 6 5 - places tile with id 3 on position 6 5 of you quilt board. The tile will be mirrored and rotated 2 time clockwise.
Constraints
Duration of first turn - 1000ms
Duration of game turn - 50ms
