The Goal

Became the master of patchworking by filling your quilt canvas with patches. Be careful! Every patch costs you a few buttons and it also takes some time to sew it on your canvas.

This game is heavily inspired by 2 player board game Patchwork.

  Rules

This is turn based game but players do not necessarily alternate between turns. The player whose time token is the furthest behind on the time board takes his turn. This may result in a player taking multiple turns in a row before his opponent can take one. If there are both time tokens on same spot, player that played last move takes his turn.

Your goal is to collect more Buttons than your opponent (Button is game currency) and to cover as much of your quilt board with patches as possible. Uncovered squares on quilt board will cost you 2 Buttons per piece at the end of game.

On your turn, you carry out one of the following actions:
  • Advance and Receive Buttons using SKIP command
  • Take and Place a Patch using TAKE command

Advance and Receive Buttons

Your time token will move on the time board so that it occupies the space directly in front of your opponent's time token. You receive 1 Button per space your time token moves. You can do so using SKIP command.

Take and Place a Patch

This action consists of a few step:
  • Choose from the three patches in front of the neutral token and place it on board
  • Pay the depicted number of buttons to the supply
  • Move your time token on the time board by a number of spaces as depicted on the label
Patches can be rotated in any way - mirrored and/or rotated right (clockwise) 0, 1, 2 or 3 times. It can be placed anywhere on board as far as it won't cover already placed patches. First player to fully cover 7x7 square of quill board will receive Special Tile (see End of the game). You can do so using TAKE command.

Time Board

Time Board consists of starting space and 53 game spaces. Regardless of the action you take, you always move your time token on the time board. Some spaces of the time board are marked. Whenever you move onto or past one of those spaces, resolve the corresponding event:
  • Special Patch: Take the special patch and place it on your quilt board. The special patches are the only way to "patch" single spaces of your quilt board. If you receive the Special Patch, you will have to resolve it in next turn.
  • Button Income: You receive a number of buttons according to the patches on your quilt board.

  End of the game

The game ends after both time tokens reach the last space of the time board. If a time token were to move past the last space, it simply stops on the last space. In case of SKIP action, you only receive buttons for the actual number of spaces moved.

Determine the number of buttons you have left, adding 7 if you own Special Tile. From this score, subtract 2 points for each empty space of your quilt board.

The player with the highest score wins. In case of a tie, the player who got to the final space of the time board first wins.

  Game Input/Output

Initialization Input

Line 1: 1 integer income_events how many "Button Income" events are there on Time Board

Line 2: income_events integers of income_time when the "Button income" event will occur

Line 3: 1 integer patch_events how many "Special Patch" events are there on Time Board

Line 4: patch_events integers of patch_time when the "Special Patch" event will occur

Line 5: tiles - count of tiles - always 33

Next tiles lines: 5 values: tile_id tile_earning tile_button_price tile_time_price tile_shape

Game turn Input

Line 1: 2 integers my_buttons = amount of buttons you currently own; and my_time_board_position = how far is your token on Time Board

Line 2: 2 integers opponent_buttons = amount of buttons your opponent currently owns; and opponent_time_board_position = how far is your opponent's token on Time Board

Line 3: 1 integer playable_tiles = amount of tiles you can play (either 3 during normal turn or 1 while resolving "Special Patch" event)

Next playable_tiles lines: 5 values: tile_id tile_earning tile_button_price tile_time_price tile_shape

To describe all fields of tile:
  • tile_id - 0 - 32 for standard tiles or -1 for Special Patch
  • tile_earning - how much will this patch earn during each "Button income" event
  • tile_button_price - how much buttons this patch costs
  • tile_time_price - how much time this patch costs
  • tile_shape - single string representing tile shape as follows: | separates rows of patch. Each rows consists of . and O characters. O means current square is taken by this patch
Output for one Game Turn
A single line with either of 2 actions:
  • SKIP
  • TAKE tile_id rotations mirror x y
Output for one Game Turn
Example of usage:
TAKE
tile_id
rotate - how many times to rotate tile clockwise
mirror - 1 if tile should be mirrored
x and y - coordinates of top/left corner after applying transformations

TAKE 3 1 1 6 5 - places tile with id 3 on position 6 5 of you quilt board. The tile will be mirrored and rotated 1 time clockwise.
Constraints
Duration of first turn - 1000ms
Duration of game turn - 50ms
